A Random Story

When Eliza inherited her grandmother’s creaky old house on the edge of town,
she wasn’t expecting much. A few dusty rooms, maybe some old photo albums,
and the lingering scent of lavender and mothballs. What she didn’t expect
was the lamp.
She found it in the attic on her second night there—a tall,
Art Deco floor lamp, tucked behind a stack of forgotten Christmas decorations
and boxes labeled “DO NOT OPEN.” Naturally, she opened them.
The lamp didn’t have a bulb. Instead, it held a glowing marble suspended in
a spiral of brass wires. It wasn’t connected to anything, and yet it pulsed faintly,
like a heartbeat. When Eliza touched it, she swore she heard a whisper—not quite a word,
but a suggestion. A feeling.
That night, her dreams were different. Vivid. She saw the house as it
used to be, full of life and music and people in elegant clothes.
Her grandmother was there, younger than Eliza had ever seen her,
standing beside the lamp. She was smiling... and whispering to it.
In the morning, the attic was warmer. Brighter. And the lamp? Still glowing,
just a little more. Eliza never figured out what powered it, or why
it whispered, or why—every time she had a question—it seemed to show her
the answer in a dream. But she stopped wondering after a while.
She just kept the lamp near her reading chair, and every now and then, she'd whisper back.
